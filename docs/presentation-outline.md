# Solleron Presentation Outline

## Audience
Mixed audience: investors/stakeholders, internal team, and potential community partners.

## Presentation Objective
Explain the problem, the marketplace plus local currency solution, why it matters for the community, and how the 2-3 month POC validates the model.

## Slide-by-Slide Structure
### 1) Title and Vision
- Solleron: Community marketplace powered by local currency.
- One-line mission: keep value local and strengthen community exchange.

### 2) Problem and Opportunity
- Local businesses lack visibility and recurring local demand.
- Residents lack one trusted place to discover and transact locally.
- Opportunity: digital platform + local incentives to shift spending behavior.

### 3) Proposed Solution
- Community marketplace connecting buyers, sellers, and organizers.
- Local currency layer rewarding participation and local trade.
- Transparent ledger and lightweight governance for pilot trust.

### 4) How the Local Currency Works
- Earn mechanics (purchase participation, campaign rewards, approved contributions).
- Spend mechanics (eligible checkout flows in marketplace).
- Governance basics (issuance rules, monitoring, abuse prevention controls).

### 5) User Journeys
- Buyer: onboarding -> browse -> buy -> earn/spend local currency.
- Vendor: onboarding -> create listing -> receive order -> receive value.
- Admin: monitor activity -> tune issuance rules -> review health metrics.

### 6) Community and Business Impact
- Potential increase in local transaction volume.
- Better vendor discoverability and customer retention.
- Community identity and engagement through local-value circulation.

### 7) POC Scope and Timeline
- 2-3 month plan with staged milestones:
  - Foundations,
  - End-to-end flows,
  - Pilot validation.
- Focused scope to prove utility, adoption, and reliability.

### 8) Risks and Mitigation
- Low currency liquidity -> targeted incentive campaigns.
- Vendor onboarding friction -> simplified onboarding playbook.
- Abuse/fraud risk -> transaction limits, monitoring alerts, admin controls.
- Regulatory uncertainty -> explicit POC boundary and legal review checkpoints.

### 9) Success Metrics
- Vendor and buyer activation.
- Transaction volume and repeat usage.
- Local currency utilization rate.
- Reliability indicators (error rates, incident trends).

### 10) Ask and Next Decisions
- Confirm pilot scope and governance assumptions.
- Approve POC execution resources.
- Align on decision criteria for post-POC scale-up.

## Suggested Storytelling Notes
- Keep language non-technical on concept slides.
- Reserve technical details for implementation and risk slides.
- Use one concrete scenario to demonstrate user value end-to-end.
- Close with measurable outcomes rather than abstract ambition.
